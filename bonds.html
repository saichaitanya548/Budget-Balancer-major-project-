Types of Bonds and Their Benefits

Government Bonds

Government bonds are low-risk investments backed by the government.

CAGR: 2-4% depending on the type (2018-2023)

Risk: Very low

Examples:
US Treasury Bonds - CAGR: 3.5%, Risk: Very low
UK Gilts - CAGR: 2.8%, Risk: Very low

Corporate Bonds

Corporate bonds are issued by companies and offer higher returns than government bonds.

CAGR: 4-6% over the past 5 years (2018-2023)

Risk: Low to moderate

Examples:
Apple Inc. Bonds - CAGR: 5.1%, Risk: Low
Microsoft Corp. Bonds - CAGR: 5.3%, Risk: Low

Municipal Bonds

Municipal bonds are issued by local governments and offer tax advantages.

CAGR: 3-5% over the past 5 years (2018-2023)

Risk: Low

Examples:
California GO Bonds - CAGR: 4.0%, Risk: Low
New York City Bonds - CAGR: 3.8%, Risk: Low

Savings Bonds

Savings bonds are government-backed and very safe investments for individuals.

CAGR: 1-3% over the past 5 years (2018-2023)

Risk: Very low

Examples:
Series I Savings Bonds - CAGR: 2.0%, Risk: Very low
Series EE Savings Bonds - CAGR: 1.5%, Risk: Very low

Investment Grade Bonds

Investment grade bonds are high-quality bonds with low default risk.

CAGR: 3-5% over the past 5 years (2018-2023)

Risk: Low

Examples:
Johnson & Johnson Bonds - CAGR: 4.2%, Risk: Low
Procter & Gamble Bonds - CAGR: 4.1%, Risk: Low

Junk Bonds

Junk bonds offer high returns but come with higher risk of default.

CAGR: 6-8% over the past 5 years (2018-2023)

Risk: High

Examples:
Ford Motor Company Bonds - CAGR: 7.0%, Risk: High
Delta Air Lines Bonds - CAGR: 6.8%, Risk: High
